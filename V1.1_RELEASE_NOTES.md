### 1. Portfolio Optimizer - Maximum Sharpe Ratio

**What It Does:**  
Automatically finds the optimal weight allocation to maximize your portfolio's Sharpe ratio using your selected ETFs.

**How It Works:**
- Uses 5,000-10,000 iteration grid search
- Tests thousands of weight combinations
- Finds the allocation that maximizes risk-adjusted returns

**Optimization Scenarios:**

1. **Maximum Sharpe Ratio (Unconstrained)**
   - Finds absolute best Sharpe ratio
   - May result in concentrated positions
   - Best for: Maximum efficiency

2. **Maximum Sharpe (Min 5% per holding)**
   - Each holding gets at least 5%
   - Maximum 40% per holding
   - Best for: Balanced optimization

3. **Minimum Variance**
   - Lowest possible volatility
   - Conservative allocation
   - Best for: Risk-averse investors

4. **Equal Risk Contribution**
   - Each holding contributes equally to risk
   - Inverse volatility weighting
   - Best for: Risk parity strategies

5. **Maximum Return (For Given Risk)**
   - Maximizes return while keeping volatility at current level
   - Maintains your risk budget
   - Best for: Improving existing portfolios

**How to Use:**

1. Build your portfolio with 2+ holdings
2. Click **"ğŸ¯ Optimize Portfolio"** in Analysis view
3. Wait 2-3 seconds for calculations
4. Review 5 optimization scenarios
5. Click "Apply These Weights" to use an optimized allocation
6. Export results to CSV for comparison

**Example Output:**

### 2. Market Regime Detection

**What It Does:**  
Analyzes current market conditions and tells you what type of market environment you're in.

**Regime Types Detected:**

1. **Bull Market - Low Volatility**
   - Strong uptrend, calm markets
   - Best for: Risk-taking, momentum, growth

2. **Bull Market - High Volatility**
   - Positive trend but choppy
   - Best for: Quality, selective momentum

3. **Sideways Market - Low Volatility**
   - Range-bound, mean-reverting
   - Best for: Value, dividend, quality

4. **Bear Market - High Volatility**
   - Downtrend, high fear
   - Best for: Defensive, bonds, low volatility

5. **Mixed Signals**
   - Unclear direction
   - Best for: Balanced diversification

**Metrics Analyzed:**

- **Market Trend**: 1-year annualized return direction
- **Volatility**: Current vs historical volatility
- **Volatility Trend**: Rising or falling uncertainty
- **Price vs 50-Day SMA**: Short-term trend
- **Price vs 200-Day SMA**: Long-term trend
- **Skewness**: Return distribution asymmetry

**Factor Performance Scores:**

For each regime, shows expected performance of:
- Market, Growth, Value, Size factors
- Momentum, Quality, Low Volatility
- Dividend, International
- Tech, Financials, Energy, Healthcare
- Real Estate, Bonds, Gold
